About Me

I am a PhD student in the Nonlinear Controls and Robotics group at the University of Florida, advised by Prof. Warren E. Dixon. My research bridges nonlinear control theory and modern machine learning.

I'm broadly interested in understanding learning systems through the lens of dynamical systems and Lyapunov stability. My work applies control-theoretic tools to problems in optimization, Bayesian estimation, and model compression, aiming for methods that are both principled and practical.

Before joining UF for my PhD, I earned an M.S. in Computer Science from Johns Hopkins University and an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UF. I received my B.S. from Tongji University in Shanghai.

Research Projects

Riemannian Lyapunov Optimizer

A unified control-theoretic framework that reinterprets optimization as a dynamical system on a Riemannian manifold, recovering SGD, Adam, AdamW, and Lion as special cases of a single Lyapunov-stable construction.

Lyapunov Theory Riemannian Geometry Optimization

Goal Inference via RBPF

A Rao-Blackwellized Particle Filter for real-time intent inference of mobile agents, leveraging Lyapunov stability assumptions to unlock analytic tractability and efficient Bayesian estimation.

Particle Filtering Bayesian Inference Intent Estimation

Effective Model Pruning

A universal, architecture-agnostic pruning method using the effective sample size (inverse Simpson index) on the probability simplex, with provable bounds on preserved performance across MLPs, CNNs, Transformers, and LLMs.

Model Compression Pruning Simplex Geometry

Lyapunov-Based KAN Adaptive Control

The first Lyapunov-based adaptive control method using Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks for uncertain nonlinear systems, enabling interpretable functional decomposition with real-time learning guarantees.

Adaptive Control KAN Lyapunov

Off-Dynamics RL (DARAIL)

Domain adaptation combined with reward-augmented imitation learning for transferring policies across environments with dynamics mismatch, with theoretical error bounds under dynamics shift.

Reinforcement Learning Domain Adaptation Imitation Learning
